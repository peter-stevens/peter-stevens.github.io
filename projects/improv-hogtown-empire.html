Hogtown Empire

In January 2013, I was invited by Bad Dog Theatre to direct an improvised series about Toronto in the 1920s.

I proposed five episodes of a high society family sliding into the slums. An improvised dark comedy.

More importantly, Artistic Director Julie Dumais assembled my dream cast: Kayla Lorette, Mark Little, Dan Beirne, Daryn McIntyre, Kirsten Rasmussen and Lindsay Mullan made up the core with guests like Alex Tindal, Becky Johnson, and Kathleen Phillips.

One of the nicest reactions during the series was an audience member who shared her disbelief with me after the show. She was shocked to have seen so many talented people, who seemed like they could carry a film or TV series, at the Comedy Bar in Toronto.

We built the show on research from the Torontoist, BlogTO, and the Toronto Star's archives. My vision was to create a historical period beyond the typical 1920s images of Chicago-style prohibition gangsters and rum running. Our Toronto was about an emerging city and the immigrants caught in the grind, living in The Ward.

The show ran for five episodes in January and February of 2013.
